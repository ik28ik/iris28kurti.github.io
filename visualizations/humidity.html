Humidity

Humidity is the concentration of water vapour present in the air. Water vapour, the gaseous state of water, is generally invisible to the human eye. Humidity indicates the likelihood for precipitation, dew, or fog to be present.

Humidity plays an important role for surface life. For animal life dependent on perspiration (sweating) to regulate internal body temperature, high humidity impairs heat exchange efficiency by reducing the rate of moisture evaporation from skin surfaces. The most humid cities on earth are generally located closer to the equator, near coastal regions. While humidity itself is a climate variable, it also overpowers other climate variables. The humidity is affected by winds and by rainfall. [1]

As we can notice from the plot, there is no obvious correlation between latitude and humidity.
